# Practice Sources

References for cultural practices: rituals, ceremonies, festivals, daily practices, art practices, martial traditions.

## Ritual and Ceremony

- Bell, Catherine. *Ritual: Perspectives and Dimensions*. Oxford: Oxford University Press, 1997.
  Practice categorization framework (ritual, daily, ceremony, festival). Sacred/secular classification.

- Turner, Victor. *The Ritual Process: Structure and Anti-Structure*. Chicago: Aldine, 1969.
  Lifecycle rituals, communitas, liminal states.

## Festivals and Calendar Practices

- Bowdin, Glenn, et al. *Events Management*. 4th ed. London: Routledge, 2012.
  Festival frequency classification (annual, seasonal, lifecycle).

## Martial and Physical Practices

- Green, Thomas A., ed. *Martial Arts of the World: An Encyclopedia*. 2 vols. Santa Barbara: ABC-CLIO, 2001.
  Martial art traditions as cultural practice, geographic origins.

## Dietary and Food Practices

- Counihan, Carole, and Penny Van Esterik, eds. *Food and Culture: A Reader*. 3rd ed. New York: Routledge, 2013.
  Food as cultural practice, sacred dietary laws, fasting traditions.

## Hip-Hop Practices

- Chang (2005). The cypher, block party, breaking, graffiti writing as cultural practice.
- Schloss, Joseph G. *Foundation: B-Boys, B-Girls, and Hip-Hop Culture in New York*. Oxford: Oxford University Press, 2009.
  Breaking as art practice, frequency (spontaneous), cultural context.

## Japanese Practices

- Varley (2000). Tea ceremony (chadō), calligraphy (shodō), flower arrangement (ikebana).
- Friday, Karl F. *Legacies of the Sword: The Kashima-Shinryū and Samurai Martial Culture*. Honolulu: University of Hawaii Press, 1997.
  Martial practice (budō) as sacred and daily cultural practice.

## Vedic/Hindu Practices

- Flood, Gavin. *An Introduction to Hinduism*. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 1996.
  Puja, yajna, dharmic daily practices. Sacred/secular distinction in Hindu context.
