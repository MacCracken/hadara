# Text Sources

References for sacred and canonical texts: composition dates, authorship, cultural significance.

## General

- Smith (1991). Canonical texts across world religions, dating conventions.
- Eliade (1959). Sacred text as axis mundi, role in cultural identity.

## Egyptian Texts

- Faulkner, R. O. *The Ancient Egyptian Pyramid Texts*. Oxford: Oxford University Press, 1969.
  Pyramid Texts (c. 2400 BCE), Book of the Dead (c. 1550 BCE).

- Allen, James P. *The Ancient Egyptian Pyramid Texts*. Atlanta: Society of Biblical Literature, 2005.
  Updated translations and dating.

## Persian / Zoroastrian Texts

- Boyce (2001). Avesta dating, Gathas (c. 1500--1000 BCE disputed), Zoroastrian canon.
- Skjaervø, Prods Oktor. "Avestan and Old Persian Morphology." In *Morphologies of Asia and Africa*, ed. Alan S. Kaye. Winona Lake: Eisenbrauns, 2007.
  Textual dating via linguistic analysis.

## Vedic / Hindu Texts

- Witzel, Michael. "Vedas and Upanisads." In *The Blackwell Companion to Hinduism*, ed. Gavin Flood. Oxford: Blackwell, 2003.
  Rigveda (c. 1500--1200 BCE), Upanishads (c. 800--200 BCE) dating.

- Flood (1996). Bhagavad Gita dating (c. 200 BCE--200 CE), Mahabharata composition.

## Japanese Texts

- Keene (1976--1999). Kojiki (712 CE), Tale of Genji (c. 1000 CE).
- Philippi, Donald L. *Kojiki*. Tokyo: University of Tokyo Press, 1968.
  Translation and dating of Japan's oldest chronicle.

## West African Texts

- Vansina (1985). Oral epics as canonical texts, Sundiata epic, dating challenges.
- Hale, Thomas A. *Griots and Griottes: Masters of Words and Music*. Bloomington: Indiana University Press, 1998.
  Oral corpus as textual tradition.

## Norse Texts

- Larrington, Carolyne, trans. *The Poetic Edda*. Oxford: Oxford University Press, 2014.
  Poetic Edda (13th c. compilation, older oral sources), Prose Edda (Snorri, c. 1220 CE).
