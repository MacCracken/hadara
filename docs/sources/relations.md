# Relation Sources

References for cultural relationships: influence, syncretism, exchange, conflict, branching, absorption.

## General Frameworks

- Bentley, Jerry H. *Old World Encounters: Cross-Cultural Contacts and Exchanges in Pre-Modern Times*. Oxford: Oxford University Press, 1993.
  Framework for classifying cultural interactions: trade, conquest, missionary activity, diffusion.

- Curtin, Philip D. *Cross-Cultural Trade in World History*. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 1984.
  Trade as vehicle for cultural exchange, Silk Road, Indian Ocean networks.

## Syncretism

- Stewart, Charles, and Rosalind Shaw, eds. *Syncretism/Anti-Syncretism: The Politics of Religious Synthesis*. London: Routledge, 1994.
  Syncretism as cultural process, Hermes Trismegistus (Greek-Egyptian), Greco-Buddhism.

## Influence and Diffusion

- Diamond (1997). Technology diffusion, cultural borrowing vs. independent invention.
- Stearns (2001). Cross-cultural influence timelines.

## Conflict and Suppression

- Said, Edward W. *Culture and Imperialism*. New York: Knopf, 1993.
  Cultural conflict, suppression, colonial impact on indigenous cultures.

## Persian-Egyptian Exchange

- Brosius (2006). Achaemenid conquest of Egypt (525 BCE), cultural exchange under Persian rule.
- Briant, Pierre. *From Cyrus to Alexander: A History of the Persian Empire*. Winona Lake: Eisenbrauns, 2002.
  Administrative and cultural exchange during Persian rule of Egypt (525--404 BCE).

## Silk Road Exchange

- Hansen, Valerie. *The Silk Road: A New History*. Oxford: Oxford University Press, 2012.
  Multi-directional cultural exchange: Chinese, Persian, Indian, Greco-Roman.

## Hip-Hop Lineage

- Rose (1994). African American musical tradition → hip-hop. Jamaican sound system culture → DJ Kool Herc.
- Chang (2005). Cross-cultural spread: New York → global. Influence from funk, soul, reggae, spoken word.
