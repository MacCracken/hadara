# Art Form Sources

References for art forms, media classification, and cultural art traditions.

## General

- Nettl et al. (1998--2002). Music traditions worldwide. Primary reference for music art forms.

- Dissanayake, Ellen. *Homo Aestheticus: Where Art Comes From and Why*. Seattle: University of Washington Press, 1995.
  Art as human universal, medium classification framework.

## Oral Tradition

- Ong, Walter J. *Orality and Literacy*. London: Methuen, 1982.
  Oral tradition as art form, distinction from written literature.

- Vansina, Jan. *Oral Tradition as History*. Madison: University of Wisconsin Press, 1985.
  African oral traditions, griot tradition, epic poetry.

## Dance

- Schechner, Richard. *Performance Studies: An Introduction*. 3rd ed. London: Routledge, 2013.
  Dance as cultural performance, ritual dance vs. secular performance.

## Architecture

- Fletcher, Banister. *A History of Architecture*. 20th ed. Oxford: Architectural Press, 1996.
  Architecture as cultural expression, regional building traditions.

## Calligraphy

- Safadi, Yasin Hamid. *Islamic Calligraphy*. London: Thames and Hudson, 1978.
  Arabic/Islamic calligraphy tradition.

- Nakata, Yujiro. *The Art of Japanese Calligraphy*. New York: Weatherhill, 1973.
  Shodō tradition and its cultural context.

## Hip-Hop Art Forms

- Rose (1994). MCing, DJing as music art forms. Graffiti as visual art.
- Fricke, Jim, and Charlie Ahearn. *Yes Yes Y'all: The Experience Music Project Oral History of Hip-Hop's First Decade*. Cambridge: Da Capo Press, 2002.
  Turntablism, beatboxing as distinct art forms.
